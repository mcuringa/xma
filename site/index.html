Educational technology is an interdisciplinary field found at the intersection of learning sciences, media studies, computer science & information systems, and teaching.

Two Tracks

The teaching track certifies existing teachers for a second certification as a NYS Educational Technology Specialist. They prepare to integrate the latest technologies into their current teaching or to move into school-wide or district-wide technology positions.

The design track prepares candidates for careers in educational technology in higher education, corporate or public learning, and educational media/software design and production.

Study

Learning Sciences

We study how people learn. We want to know about how the mind works, processing knowledge. We also study how people learn together, in interaction with the world.

Design

We educators engage in many types of design: curriculum design, interaction design, game design, multimedia design, data visualization, lesson design, software design, and more. We advance our design thinking and skills through hands-on studios and projects.

Hacking

In programming, hacking emphasizes the creative, playful side of writing code; with an emphasis on seeing beyond apparent limitations. We study programming, networking, and hardware so we know how to change them.
